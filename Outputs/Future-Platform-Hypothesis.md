# Future Platform Hypothesis

## Purpose

This document outlines preliminary hypotheses regarding the characteristics of a lightweight clinical data infrastructure that could improve the visibility of routine healthcare data in low-resource settings.

These hypotheses are not validated requirements. They are assumptions to be explored and refined through the pilot activities described in this project.

The findings from workflow mapping, staff interviews, and data capture testing will be used to confirm, modify, or reject these assumptions.


# Core Hypothesis

A lightweight, workflow-aligned data capture system can significantly improve the visibility of routine clinical information in low-resource healthcare settings without requiring deployment of a full electronic health record system.


# Research Questions

The pilot seeks to investigate:

1. Where does clinical information become lost or inaccessible during routine care?
2. What information is already being collected but not digitized?
3. What operational constraints limit structured data capture?
4. What level of standardization is feasible within existing workflows?
5. What minimal infrastructure could improve data visibility without increasing burden on healthcare workers?


# Preliminary Business Requirements (Hypotheses)

Based on current understanding, a future system should:

### BR-01: Minimize Documentation Burden

The system should fit within existing clinical workflows and avoid duplicative data entry.

### BR-02: Function in Resource-Constrained Environments

The system should be usable in facilities with limited infrastructure and inconsistent connectivity.

### BR-03: Support Low Digital Literacy

Interfaces and workflows should be simple and require minimal technical training.

### BR-04: Generate Structured Data

Routine clinical encounters should produce data that can be aggregated and analyzed.

### BR-05: Remain Affordable

Implementation and maintenance costs should be compatible with resource-constrained health systems.


# Preliminary Functional Requirements (Hypotheses)

Based on current assumptions, a future system may need to support:

### FR-01: Patient Registration

Capture basic demographic information.

### FR-02: Encounter Documentation

Record clinical encounters in a structured format.

### FR-03: Longitudinal Patient Tracking

Link multiple visits to the same patient when feasible.

### FR-04: Data Export

Allow data extraction for reporting, analysis, and research purposes.

### FR-05: Basic Reporting

Support generation of routine summaries and indicators.


# Preliminary Technical Requirements (Hypotheses)

Based on anticipated operational realities, a future system may require:

### TR-01: Offline Functionality

Operation during periods of limited or absent internet connectivity.

### TR-02: Open Standards Compatibility

Support interoperability through widely adopted standards where appropriate.

### TR-03: Secure Data Storage

Protect sensitive information through appropriate access controls and storage practices.

### TR-04: Data Portability

Enable export of structured datasets for secondary use.

### TR-05: Low-Cost Deployment

Function on affordable hardware commonly available in low-resource settings.


# Evidence to Be Collected During the Pilot

The following evidence will be used to evaluate these hypotheses:

* Workflow observations
* Clinical process mapping
* Staff interviews
* Data completeness assessments
* Documentation burden observations
* Pilot data capture results


# Expected Outcome

At the conclusion of the pilot, this document will be updated to produce an evidence-informed set of recommendations regarding the business, functional, and technical characteristics of future lightweight health data infrastructure.

The objective is not to produce a full system specification, but to establish a foundation for future research and implementation efforts aimed at improving representation in global health datasets.
