# Clinical Data Flow Diagram Template

## Purpose

This template is used to map how routine clinical events are transformed into health data within healthcare facilities, and to identify where information is lost, fragmented, delayed, or rendered unusable for secondary purposes such as:
* public health surveillance
* epidemiological monitoring
* health system reporting
* AI-enabled health analytics and forecasting
The focus is on understanding the transition from clinical activity to structured, reusable data.

## For Each Step, Document:


1. Responsible Actor

Who performs the action?

* Clinical staff
* Administrative staff
* Data clerk
* Mixed responsibility
  

2. Data Generated
   
What information is produced at this step?

Examples:
* Demographics
* Symptoms
* Clinical observations
* Diagnosis codes or free text
* Treatment records
* Laboratory requests/results


3. Data Format
   
How is information recorded?

* Paper-based
* Digital system
* Hybrid
* Verbal / not formally recorded
  

4. Storage Location
   
Where is the information stored?

* Patient file
* Registry book
* Department logbook
* Local device
* Central system
* Not stored / transient
  

5. Data Transfer Mechanism
   
How does information move to the next step or system?

* Manual transcription
* Paper transfer
* Digital entry
* Reporting forms
* Informal communication
  

6. Risk of Data Loss or Distortion
   
At this step, assess:
* Loss of information
* Simplification or summarization
* Duplication
* Delays in recording
* Breakdown of linkage across steps
  

7. Data Visibility for Secondary Use
   
Assess whether data from this step is:
* Available for reporting
* Usable for epidemiological analysis
* Structured enough for aggregation
* Potentially usable for AI or surveillance systems

Key Output of This Template

* Identification of where clinical information becomes non-reusable data
* Mapping of bottlenecks in data generation and flow
* Identification of opportunities for lightweight structured capture
* Understanding of how routine care translates (or fails to translate) into surveillance- and AI-relevant datasets
