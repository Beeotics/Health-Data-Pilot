# Capture-to-Dataset Workflow

## Objective

The pilot seeks to understand how routine clinical information can move from everyday care activities into structured datasets suitable for:

* public health surveillance
* epidemiological monitoring
* health system planning
* AI-based health analytics and forecasting

The focus is on identifying how clinical information is transformed (or fails to be transformed) into structured, reusable data within real-world healthcare workflows.


## Workflow

Patient Encounter

↓

Clinical Observation

↓

Paper Register / Clinical Notes

↓

Information Loss / Fragmentation Points

↓

Lightweight Structured Capture

↓

Data Validation and Standardization

↓

Structured Dataset Generation

↓

Data Quality and Completeness Assessment

↓

Reporting, Surveillance Use, and Secondary Analysis

↓

Recommendations for Scale-Up of Data Infrastructure


## Key Research Question

At which points in this workflow does clinical information become:

* incomplete
* inconsistent
* delayed
* unlinked
* unavailable for secondary use in surveillance, research, or AI systems


## Expected Pilot Contribution

The pilot will identify practical, low-cost interventions that improve the visibility, structure, and usability of routine clinical data, while remaining feasible in low-resource healthcare environments.

These findings will inform the design of data workflows capable of supporting:

* biosurveillance systems
* epidemiological analysis
* AI model development and evaluation
* health system intelligence
