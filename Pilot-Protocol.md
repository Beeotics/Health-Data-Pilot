# Pilot Protocol

## Title

Reducing Clinical Data Invisibility for AI and Biosurveillance: A Lightweight Data Capture Pilot in Kinshasa


## Objective

To identify where routine clinical information becomes invisible within healthcare workflows and to evaluate whether a lightweight structured data capture approach can improve the generation of reusable health data for research, biosurveillance, and future AI applications.


## Study Duration

8–12 Weeks


## Study Setting

Selected healthcare facilities in Kinshasa, Democratic Republic of the Congo.

Facility selection will be based on:

* Willingness to participate
* Feasibility of access
* Diversity of clinical workflows
* Potential to observe routine data management practices


## Study Rationale

Modern AI systems, epidemiological forecasting tools, and biosurveillance platforms depend on structured health datasets. However, many healthcare facilities in low-resource settings generate clinical information that never becomes part of broader data ecosystems.

This pilot investigates the operational barriers that prevent routine clinical information from becoming structured, reusable data and evaluates whether lightweight data capture approaches can reduce data invisibility in real-world healthcare settings.


## Study Activities

### Activity 1: Clinical Workflow Mapping

Document how patient information moves through healthcare facilities.

Assessment areas:

* Patient registration
* Clinical documentation
* Reporting pathways
* Data aggregation processes
* Information transfer between departments

Outputs:

* Workflow diagrams
* Process descriptions
* Identification of data loss points
* Data invisibility assessment


### Activity 2: Healthcare Worker Interviews

Conduct semi-structured interviews with:

* Physicians
* Nurses
* Administrative staff
* Data personnel

Topics:

* Documentation practices
* Reporting requirements
* Data management challenges
* Perceived value of structured data collection
* Operational constraints

Target:

10–20 interviews


### Activity 3: Structured Data Capture Pilot

Implement and test a lightweight structured data capture workflow integrated into routine clinical practice.

Potential fields:

* Patient demographics
* Visit date
* Chief complaint
* Diagnosis
* Treatment
* Follow-up status
* Facility identifier

Target:

300–1000 patient encounters (depending on feasibility)

Evaluation focus:

* Practical feasibility
* Staff acceptability
* Data completeness
* Data standardization
* Potential for aggregation and secondary use


### Activity 4: Data Quality and Visibility Assessment

Assess:

* Completeness
* Consistency
* Standardization
* Timeliness
* Ease of aggregation
* Potential interoperability

Additional assessment:

* Points where information becomes unavailable for research, biosurveillance, or future AI-relevant datasets.


## Data Management

All collected information will be:

* De-identified
* Stored securely
* Access-controlled
* Used exclusively for project purposes

No personally identifiable information will be included in public outputs.

Any dataset released publicly will contain only anonymized and aggregated information.


## Deliverables

### Clinical Data Flow Map

Visual representation of information movement through participating facilities.

### Field Report

Documentation of:

* Existing workflows
* Data invisibility points
* Operational barriers
* Feasibility findings
* Recommendations

### Structured Dataset Sample

An anonymized dataset demonstrating the feasibility of lightweight routine data capture.

### Reusable Data Capture Template

A framework that can be adapted in similar healthcare environments.

### Recommendations Document

Practical recommendations for improving routine health data visibility and participation in broader health data ecosystems.


## Success Criteria

The pilot will be considered successful if:

* Clinical workflows are mapped in participating facilities
* Data invisibility points are identified and documented
* Healthcare worker interviews are completed
* A structured pilot dataset is generated
* Data quality assessment is completed
* Final report is delivered within the project timeline
* Practical recommendations are produced

Success will be measured by the project's ability to generate actionable evidence on how routine clinical information can become more visible and reusable within the data ecosystems that increasingly support public health intelligence, biosurveillance, and AI-enabled health systems.
