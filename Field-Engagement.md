# Field Engagement Summary

## Purpose

To assess the feasibility and relevance of the proposed health data pilot, informal discussions were conducted with healthcare stakeholders in Kinshasa. These engagements helped validate operational assumptions, identify practical constraints, and refine the pilot design.

## Stakeholder Engagement Overview

| Stakeholder Type              | Setting                   | Engagement Status            | Key Insights                                                                       |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------- | ---------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Physician                     | Urban primary care clinic | Initial discussion completed | Confirmed need for improved patient data organization and reporting.               |
| Director and physician        | Outpatient facility       | Feedback collected           | Highlighted challenges related to paper-based record keeping and data consistency. |
| Physician                     | Community health facility | Concept reviewed             | Expressed interest in tools that support routine monitoring and service reporting. |
| Physician                     | Urban primary care clinic | Initial discussion completed | Emphasized the importance of simple data collection workflows.                     |

## Main Findings

The discussions indicated a shared interest in practical approaches to health data management that:

* Reduce reliance on fragmented paper records.
* Improve consistency of routine data collection.
* Support basic reporting and service monitoring.
* Remain feasible within resource-constrained environments.

## Influence on Pilot Design

Stakeholder feedback informed several aspects of the pilot:

* Development of a simplified data structure.
* Inclusion of standardized data fields.
* Use of synthetic data for prototype development and testing.
* Consideration of ethical and privacy safeguards from the earliest stages.

## Current Status

Field engagement remains ongoing. Future phases of the project will include additional consultations and formal pilot partnerships, subject to funding and institutional approvals.
