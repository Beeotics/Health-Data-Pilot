# Semi-Structured Interview Guide

## Project

Clinical Data Invisibility in Kinshasa: Understanding Barriers to Routine Health Data Capture


# Introduction

Thank you for participating.

The purpose of this interview is to understand how clinical information is recorded, managed, and used in everyday healthcare practice.

We are not evaluating individual performance. We are interested in understanding systems, workflows, and challenges.

Participation is voluntary.

# Section 1: Current Documentation Practices

1. How do you typically document patient encounters?

2. What records are maintained during routine care?

3. Are records primarily paper-based or digital?

4. What information is most important to record?


# Section 2: Challenges

5. What are the biggest challenges in documenting patient information?

6. Are there situations where information is not recorded?

7. What causes information to be lost or incomplete?

8. Are duplicate records common?


# Section 3: Reporting and Data Use

9. What reports are regularly required?

10. Who uses the data collected?

11. Do you believe the collected information is useful?

12. What information would you like to capture but currently cannot?


# Section 4: Digital Systems

13. Have you used digital health systems before?

14. What worked well?

15. What challenges did you experience?

16. What would make data collection easier?


# Section 5: Future Improvements

17. If you could change one thing about the current system, what would it be?

18. What would help improve data quality?

19. What would encourage staff to maintain structured records?

20. Any additional comments?
