# Semi-Structured Interview Guide

## Project

Reducing Clinical Data Invisibility for AI and Biosurveillance: Understanding Barriers to Routine Health Data Capture in Kinshasa


## Introduction

Thank you for participating.

The purpose of this interview is to understand how clinical information is recorded, managed, reported, and used in everyday healthcare practice.

We are not evaluating individual performance. We are interested in understanding systems, workflows, challenges, and opportunities for improving the visibility and usability of routine health data.

Participation is voluntary.

# Section 1: Current Documentation Practices

1. How do you typically document patient encounters?

2. What records are maintained during routine care?

3. Are records primarily paper-based, digital, or a combination of both?

4. What information is considered most important to record?

5. Are there any differences in documentation practices between departments or services?


# Section 2: Data Flow and Information Movement

1. What happens to patient information after it is recorded?

2. How is information shared within the facility?

3. How is information reported to district, provincial, or national authorities?

4. Are there stages where information must be copied, transferred, or summarized?

5. Where do you think information is most likely to be lost, delayed, or become incomplete?


# Section 3: Challenges and Data Invisibility

1. What are the biggest challenges in documenting patient information?

2. Are there situations where information is not recorded?

3. What factors contribute to missing or incomplete records?

4. Are duplicate records common?

5. Are there types of information that are routinely collected but difficult to retrieve later?

6. Are there data that you believe should be available for reporting or analysis but currently are not?


# Section 4: Reporting and Data Use

1. What reports are regularly required?

2. Who uses the information collected?

3. How useful do you believe the current reporting process is?

4. Have you ever seen collected data influence decisions, planning, or resource allocation?

5. What information would you like decision-makers to have access to that is currently unavailable?


# Section 5: Digital Systems and Structured Data

1. Have you used digital health systems before?

2. What worked well?

3. What challenges did you experience?

4. What would make routine data collection easier?

5. What would make structured data entry more feasible in your daily work?

6. What concerns would you have about introducing a simple structured data capture system?


# Section 6: Health Data Visibility and Future Uses

1. Do you think the information collected in your facility accurately reflects the health problems seen in your patients?

2. Are there important patient populations or conditions that may be underrepresented in existing reports?

3. What types of information would be most valuable for public health monitoring?

4. What changes would make clinical information more useful for research, disease surveillance, or health system planning?

5. What would help ensure that routine clinical information is not lost and remains available for future use?


# Section 7: Future Improvements

1. If you could change one aspect of the current documentation system, what would it be?

2. What would help improve data quality?

3. What would encourage staff to maintain structured records?

4. What would make reporting easier?

5. Any additional comments or recommendations?
